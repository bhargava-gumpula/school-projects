Weather

The typical weather in Greece varies depending on the region and time of year. Greece generally experiences a
Mediterranean climate, which is characterized by mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers.


In the summer months (June to August), temperatures in Greece can range from 30°C to 40°C (86°F to 104°F) or
even higher, especially in the interior and on the southern islands. The coastal areas are usually a bit cooler due to
sea breezes. This is also the driest period, with very little rainfall.


In the winter months (December to February), temperatures are milder, ranging from 10°C to 20°C (50°F to 68°F)
depending on the region. The northern parts of the country and the mountainous areas tend to be cooler, and can
even experience snowfall. The southern regions and islands are generally warmer and receive more rainfall during
this time.


Spring (March to May) and fall (September to November) are considered the best times to visit Greece. The weather
is generally mild and pleasant, with temperatures ranging from 15°C to 25°C (59°F to 77°F), and there is less tourism
during these periods, making it easier to explore the country's many historic sites and natural beauty spots.
